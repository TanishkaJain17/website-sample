The Man Behind the Myths

Sundeep
Verma

Mythologist. Author. Storyteller. Founder of Cobalt Blue Foundation. A man who left advertising to spend his life decoding why ancient stories still run our lives today.

A Journey Through Stories

How Sundeep arrived at mythology

Early Career

Advertising & Brand Strategy

Leadership roles at Ogilvy, Grey, and Omnicom Group — building brand narratives for some of India's largest organisations. Learning that all communication is, at its core, storytelling.

"I was always telling stories. I just hadn't found the oldest ones yet."

London

Central Saint Martins, UAL

Trained in Narrative Environment Design — understanding how space, story, and human experience intersect. The seed of the Mythology Lab was planted here.

The Turn

From Brand Stories to Mythic Ones

Leaving the corporate world behind, Sundeep turned to the oldest stories humanity ever told — asking why they still echo inside us today. The workshops began in intimate circles.

"Once you understand that mythology is psychology, you can never un-see it."

2021

Ahalya — The Unploughed

Published his debut book — a visceral poetic retelling of one of Indian mythology's most misread women. Received as a work that "reads like a reckoning."

Present

Cobalt Blue Foundation

Founded to build mythology as a discipline for self-inquiry, cultural leadership, and institutional storytelling. 150+ workshops. 13+ cultural institutions. Still growing.

"Mythology is not religion. It is not superstition. It is the most sophisticated psychology ever created — disguised as story."

— Sundeep Verma

Philosophy

What Sundeep believes

I.

Mythology is Psychology

Every mythic character is an archetype — a crystallised pattern of human behaviour. To understand Karna is to understand ambition and wounds.

II.

Stories are Mirrors

A myth doesn't tell you what to think. It holds up a mirror and asks you to look honestly. The stories that disturb you most are often the ones most useful.

III.

Curiosity Before Belief

Mythology is not a faith system. It is a thinking system. The moment you approach it with curiosity, it becomes infinitely more powerful.

IV.

Ancient. Timeless. Now.

The Ramayana, the Mahabharata, the Puranas — they were not written for another age. They were written for this moment, for you, today.

About the Book

Mythology · Poetry · Feminine Archetypes

Ahalya is one of the Panchakanya — the five sacred women of Indian mythology. Traditionally remembered for a curse, Sundeep Verma's retelling asks a different question: what if she wasn't a victim of another's story, but the author of her own desire?

Written in flowing, sensory verse, the book moves through longing, transgression, silence, and emergence. Each illustration is not decoration — it is interpretation. Together, text and image make a case for reading ancient women on their own terms.

An ancient symbol for the contemporary woman's conflict between her mind and her heart.
